🎯 What is this?

This is a paper trading dashboard — no real money is at risk. We're testing whether a statistical model (Elo ratings) can spot mispriced bets on Polymarket, a prediction market where people buy and sell shares on sports outcomes. If our model consistently finds real edges, we'd graduate to real money.

🧠 How the model decides to trade

The system uses three-source triangulation:

  1. Elo Model — Our own rating system trained on thousands of games. It produces a win probability for each team.
  2. Polymarket price — What the betting market thinks the probability is. A share priced at $0.65 means the market thinks there's a 65% chance.
  3. Sportsbooks (Vegas) — What professional bookmakers think. Used as a tiebreaker.

We trade when our Elo model disagrees with Polymarket by more than 7%. For example: if Elo says a team has a 70% chance of winning but Polymarket prices it at 60%, that's a 10% edge — we'd buy.

📊 Reading the stats

Total P&L

If we'd bet real money, how much we'd be up or down total. Green = profit, red = loss.

Bankroll

Our simulated bankroll (started at $200). Goes up when we win, down when we lose.

Brier Score

Measures how accurate our probability predictions are. 0.00 = perfect, 0.25 = as good as flipping a coin, lower is better. Under 0.22 is considered good.

ROI (Return on Investment)

Profit divided by total amount wagered, as a percentage. Positive = we're making money on average.

📋 Reading the trades table

Dir (YES)— Betting the away team wins. In Polymarket, YES = the first team listed (away team).
Dir (NO)— Betting the home team wins. We're saying "no, the away team won't win."
Entry $— Price per share. If we buy at $0.40 and win, we get $1.00 back (profit = $0.60 per share).
Elo %— What our model thinks the true probability is that this team wins. The bigger the gap between this and the entry price, the more "edge" we think we have.
Size— How much we bet. Determined by the Kelly Criterion — bets more when the edge is bigger and our confidence is higher.

⚖️ What "sportsbooks agree with Polymarket" means

When we find an edge (our model disagrees with Polymarket), we check what Vegas thinks. There are three scenarios:

🟢Elo + Vegas agree, Polymarket disagrees — Strongest signal. Two independent sources say the market is wrong. High confidence trade.
🟡Only Elo disagrees — Our model sees something the market and Vegas don't. Could be a real edge, or our model could be wrong. Medium confidence.
🔴Polymarket + Vegas + Elo all agree — No edge. The market is efficient. We don't trade.

When we say "sportsbooks agree with Polymarket, not Elo" — it means Vegas and the betting market both think our model is wrong. We still paper trade to test if our model catches something they miss, but it's a yellow/orange flag.

👻 What are shadow trades?

Shadow trades are bets our model said NO to — the edge wasn't big enough, or the signal was too weak. We track them anyway with fake $10 bets to learn from them. If shadow trades keep winning, it means our model is being too picky and we should lower our thresholds. They don't affect the bankroll.

🏆 Elo Ratings explained

Elo is a rating system (originally from chess) where every team starts at 1500. When you beat a strong team, you gain more points; when you lose to a weak team, you lose more. After thousands of games, the ratings reflect each team's true strength. We use this to predict game outcomes independent of what the market thinks.
